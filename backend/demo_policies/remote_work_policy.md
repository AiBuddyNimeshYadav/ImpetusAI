# Remote Work / Work-From-Home Policy — Impetus Technologies

## 1. Overview
Impetus Technologies supports a **hybrid work model** that allows employees to work from home on designated days while maintaining collaborative in-office presence. This policy outlines the guidelines, eligibility, and expectations for remote work.

## 2. Eligibility
- All full-time employees who have completed their **probation period** (typically 6 months) are eligible for remote work.
- New joiners during probation must work from office unless approved by their manager and HR.
- Certain roles (e.g., IT infrastructure, facilities management) may require full-time on-site presence.
- Contractors and temporary employees follow the terms of their engagement agreement.

## 3. Hybrid Schedule
- The standard hybrid model allows **3 days remote** and **2 days in-office** per week.
- Mandatory in-office days are **Tuesday and Thursday** (subject to team manager discretion).
- Teams may define additional in-office days for collaborative activities.
- Fully remote arrangements require **VP-level approval** and HR sign-off.

## 4. Remote Work Guidelines
### 4.1 Workspace Requirements
- Employees must have a **dedicated, quiet workspace** with a stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps).
- The workspace should be free from distractions and suitable for professional video calls.
- Employees are responsible for ensuring their home office meets ergonomic standards.

### 4.2 Equipment
- The company provides a **laptop** and **headset** for remote work.
- Employees may request a **monitor, keyboard, and mouse** through IT (one-time provision).
- Internet and electricity costs for remote work are **not reimbursed** in the standard policy.
- Senior managers (Band E and above) receive a monthly **₹2,000 WFH allowance** for utilities.

### 4.3 Availability
- Remote employees must be available during **core hours: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM IST**.
- Employees should respond to messages within **30 minutes** during core hours.
- Update your Slack/Teams status to reflect availability (online, in a meeting, away).
- Block calendar for breaks or personal commitments during the workday.

### 4.4 Communication
- All team meetings and stand-ups should be attended with **camera on** (encouraged, not mandatory).
- Use Slack for quick messages, Teams for meetings, and email for formal communication.
- Document key decisions and outcomes from meetings in shared spaces (Confluence, Notion).

## 5. Performance and Accountability
- Remote work is a privilege, not a right. Performance expectations remain the same.
- Managers assess remote employees on **output and deliverables**, not hours logged.
- Regular 1-on-1s with managers (at least bi-weekly) are mandatory.
- Consistent underperformance may result in the remote work privilege being revoked.

## 6. Data Security
- Always use the **company VPN** when accessing internal systems remotely.
- Do not use public Wi-Fi for work without VPN.
- Lock your workstation when stepping away.
- Do not share screens with confidential data when non-employees are present.
- Follow all data protection policies outlined in the Information Security Policy.

## 7. Time Tracking
- Remote employees must log their working hours in the company HRMS.
- Overtime for remote work follows the same policy as in-office work.
- Employees should not work more than **9 hours per day** (excluding lunch break) unless project demands require it, with manager approval.

## 8. Health and Safety
- Employees are encouraged to take regular breaks (follow the **20-20-20 rule** for eye health).
- Report any work-related injuries or health concerns to HR.
- The company provides access to an **Employee Assistance Program (EAP)** for mental health support.
- Contact EAP: eap@impetus.com or call +91-120-XXX-XXXX (confidential).

## 9. Exceptions and Special Arrangements
- Extended remote work (e.g., care for a family member, relocation) requires a formal request to HR.
- International remote work is **not permitted** without prior approval from Legal and HR due to tax and compliance implications.
- Employees relocating to a different city must inform HR for tax documentation updates.
